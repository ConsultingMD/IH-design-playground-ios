## Shared traits across these segments

- **Motivations:** Reduce uncertainty, save time, feel respected and informed, complete concrete tasks (care, benefits, billing) without hunting across portals.
- **Frictions:** Benefits/care jargon, fragmented documents (EOBs, denials, visit summaries), surprise costs, long waits for answers, fear of doing something “wrong” with insurance or referrals.
- **Digital confidence:** Mixed—many are comfortable with everyday apps but **not** with clinical or billing vocabulary; prefer progressive disclosure and plain language.
- **Accessibility & cognitive load:** Stress and sleep deprivation (especially postpartum and caregiver personas) increase need for **scannable layouts**, consistent patterns, forgiving recovery paths, and **non-visual cues** paired with icons/avatars.

## How researchers / designers should use this in experiments

When testing a new idea, name **one primary persona** and **one scenario** (context, constraint, emotional state). Ask whether the concept increases **clarity, trust, and task success** for that person **without** confusing or excluding the others (e.g. first-time users vs power users managing deep clinical histories).
