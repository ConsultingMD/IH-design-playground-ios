# Persona — new to IH, no previous engagement

## Snapshot

- **Label:** First-time member (IH unfamiliar)
- **Goal:** Understand what Included Health *is*, what they can do today, and how to get help without feeling lost or sold to.
- **Quote:** *“I’m not sure if this is for booking doctors, insurance stuff, or something else—I just need someone to point me to the right first step.”*

## Context

- **Typical situation:** Lands from employer/benefits onboarding, an invite email, or app store; may have **no** mental model of navigation vs advocacy vs benefits support.
- **Constraints:** Short attention span, low patience for walls of text; may be comparing to past health portals that felt broken; privacy-sensitive about health data until trust builds.

## Behaviors

- **How they navigate:** Skims home/dashboard; looks for **one clear primary action** (“Get care,” “Understand my benefits,” “Talk to someone”). May bounce between tabs if labels feel synonymous.
- **What makes them bounce:** Dense acronyms (EOB, HSA, PCP, referral) without definitions; forced deep setup before any value; unclear what is human vs automated (e.g. Dot).

## Implications for design

- **Do:** Explain **value in one screen**; offer **guided entry** (intent picker); show **progress** through onboarding; make **human escalation** obvious and low-friction.
- **Avoid:** Empty states that assume prior IH knowledge; hiding core actions behind feature tours only; mixing marketing tone with urgent care language.

## Test scenarios (sandbox)

1. **Cold open:** Open app first time after benefits invite → find **one** path to “get medical help” and confirm what happens next in plain language.
2. **Trust:** User hesitates to share condition → see **why** data is requested and **what** is optional vs required before continuing.
