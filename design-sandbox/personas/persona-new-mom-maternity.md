# Persona — new mom (maternity journey support)

## Snapshot

- **Label:** Postpartum / maternity-focused member
- **Goal:** Feel supported through **pregnancy and/or fourth trimester**—physical recovery, feeding, sleep, mental health check-ins, and **safe escalation** when something feels wrong.
- **Quote:** *“I’m running on no sleep. If this app makes me hunt, I’ll close it and guess—and that scares me.”*

## Context

- **Typical situation:** Using the product between feedings or at odd hours; emotionally heightened; may be **first child** or navigating complications (NICU, BP concerns) **without** assuming either in copy.
- **Constraints:** One-handed phone use, frequent interruptions, possible **privacy** around mental health (stigma); partner may use same benefits but different tasks.

## Behaviors

- **How they navigate:** Skims for **short lists**, **clear CTAs**, and **reassuring tone**; values **“when to seek urgent care”** guardrails; may prefer chat-style guidance if it feels human and bounded.
- **What makes them bounce:** Alarmist or vague symptom content; walls of policy text; flows that don’t account for **stopped mid-task**; shamey language around feeding or mental health.

## Implications for design

- **Do:** Support **resume** and gentle reminders; pair educational content with **clear next steps**; foreground **BH / crisis resources** where appropriate without sensationalizing.
- **Avoid:** Dense clinical dumps without hierarchy; requiring long forms during acute worry; **binary** language that assumes one “right” maternity path.

## Test scenarios (sandbox)

1. **3am check-in:** User feels anxious about a symptom → finds **triage-style guidance** + **human/Dot path** + explicit **ER/urgent** cue when warranted.
2. **BH access:** User explores postpartum mood support → **private, stigma-aware** entry to behavioral health options and scheduling/messaging.
